Johan Cruijff

Football legend, innovator of Total Football

Here's a timeline of Johan Cruyff's life:

  • 1947 - Born in Amsterdam, Netherlands.
  • 1957 - Joins Ajax youth academy at age 10.
  • 1964 - Makes his first-team debut for Ajax.
  • 1966 - Wins his first Eredivisie title with Ajax.
  • 1971 - Wins his first Ballon d'Or as Europe's best player.
  • 1973 - Transfers to FC Barcelona for a world-record fee.
  • 1974 - Leads the Netherlands to the World Cup final, revolutionizing football with "Total Football." Wins his third Ballon d'Or.
  • 1978 - Retires from international football, having won 48 caps and scored 33 goals for the Netherlands.
  • 1984 - Officially retires from professional football after playing for Feyenoord.
  • 1988 - Returns to Barcelona as head coach, building the legendary "Dream Team" and winning four consecutive La Liga titles.
  • 1992 - Leads Barcelona to their first-ever European Cup victory.
  • 1996 - Leaves Barcelona but remains a key figure in their philosophy.
  • 2010 - Acts as an advisor for Ajax and Catalonia's national team.
  • 2016 - Passes away at age 68, leaving an unmatched legacy in football.
